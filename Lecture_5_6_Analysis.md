## Slide 2: Planning and Strategy
*   Organizations must deal with dynamic internal/external environments.
*   Failure to adapt is a major mistake.

## Slide 3: Decision Making & Strategy Levels
*   **Decisions vs Level**:
    *   Top Executives: Unstructured problems, Nonprogrammed decisions (Strategic Planning).
    *   Lower Managers: Structured problems, Programmed decisions (Operational Planning).
*   **Strategy Levels**:
    1.  **Corporate Strategy**: "What businesses are we in?" (Corporation level).
    2.  **Business Strategy**: "How do we compete in each major business?" (SBU level).
    3.  **Functional Strategy**: "How do we best support each of our business strategies?" (Finance, HR, Manufacturing, Marketing).

## Slide 4: The What and Why of Planning
*   **Goals (Objectives)**: Desired outcomes or targets. Guide decisions and measurement.
*   **Plans**: Documents outlining how goals are met (resource allocations, schedules).
*   **Types of Plans**:
    *   **Breadth**: Strategic vs. Operational.
    *   **Time Frame**: Long term vs. Short term.
    *   **Specificity**: Directional vs. Specific.
    *   **Frequency**: Single use vs. Standing.

## Slide 5: A Framework for Planning
*   **Hierarchy**:
    *   **Strategic Planning**: Master plan shaping destiny.
    *   **Tactical Planning**: Translates strategic plans into specific goals by unit.
    *   **Operational Planning**: Specific procedures and actions at lower levels.
*   **7-Step Process**:
    1.  Define present situation.
    2.  Establish goals and objectives.
    3.  Analyze environment (aids/barriers).
    4.  Develop action plans.
    5.  Develop budgets.
    6.  Implement the plans.
    7.  Control the plans.

## Slide 6: Strategic Management Process
1.  **Strategic Analysis** (Assessing competitive position):
    *   Identify Mission, Stakeholders, Values, Objectives.
    *   Analyze Organization (Strengths/Weaknesses).
    *   Analyze Environment (Opportunities/Threats).
2.  **Strategy Formulation** (Creating strategies):
    *   Revise objectives and select new strategies (Corporate, Business, Functional).
3.  **Strategy Implementation** (Putting strategies into action):
    *   Implement strategies (Management systems, leadership).
    *   Evaluate results (Strategic control, Corporate governance).

## Slide 7: Competitive Strategies (Porter)
*   **Matrix**: Market Coverage (Broad vs Limited) x Value Creation (Lower Cost vs Differentiation).
*   **Strategies**:
    1.  **Overall Low-Cost Provider**: Low cost, broad market.
    2.  **Broad Differentiation**: Unique product, broad market.
    3.  **Focused Low-Cost**: Low cost, narrow market.
    4.  **Focused Differentiation**: Unique product, narrow market.
    5.  **Best-Cost Provider**: Hybrid, satisfying expectations on quality/price.

## Slide 8-9: Blue Ocean Strategy (Value Innovation)
*   **Blue Ocean**:
    *   Create uncontested market space (New market creation).
    *   Make competition irrelevant.
    *   Create and capture new demand.
    *   Break the value-cost trade-off (Value Innovation).
*   **Red Ocean**:
    *   Compete in existing market.
    *   Beat the competition.
    *   Exploit existing demand.
    *   Make the value-cost trade-off.

## Slide 10: Miles and Snow Typology
*   **Prospector**: Innovative, growth-oriented, seeks new opportunities, takes risks.
*   **Defender**: Protects current markets, stable growth, serves current customers.
*   **Analyzer**: Maintains current markets while moderately innovating.
*   **Reactor**: No clear strategy, reacts to environment, drifts.

## Slide 11: Corporate Level Strategies
*   **Ansoff Matrix** (Growth Strategies):
    *   **Market Penetration**: Existing Product, Existing Market.
    *   **Market Development**: Existing Product, New Market.
    *   **Product Development**: New Product, Existing Market.
    *   **Diversification**: New Product, New Market (Related vs Unrelated).
*   **Other Strategies**:
    *   **Stability**: Pause/Proceed with caution, No Change, Profit.
    *   **Retrenchment**: Turnaround, Captive Company, Sell-Out/Divestment, Bankruptcy/Liquidation.

## Slide 12: Portfolio Analysis (BCG Matrix)
*   **Axes**: Market Growth Rate (High/Low) vs Market Share (High/Low).
*   **Quadrants**:
    *   **Stars** (High Growth, High Share): Great opportunities. **Build**.
    *   **Cash Cows** (Low Growth, High Share): Profitable, limited growth. **Hold/Milk**.
    *   **Question Marks** (High Growth, Low Share): Fast growing, consume cash, low share. **Build or Divest**.
    *   **Dogs** (Low Growth, Low Share): Weak in market. **Divest/Harvest**.

## Slide 13: Global Organizations
*   **Definitions**:
    *   **Global Village**: Boundaryless world.
    *   **MNC**: Operations in multiple countries.
    *   **Multidomestic**: Decentralized to local country (Polycentric).
    *   **Global**: Centralized in home country (Ethnocentric).
    *   **Transnational**: Borderless structure (Geocentric).
*   **Global Integration vs Local Responsiveness (Bartlett & Ghoshal)**:
    *   **Global Strategy**: High Integration, Low Responsiveness.
    *   **Transnational Strategy**: High Integration, High Responsiveness.
    *   **International Strategy**: Low Integration, Low Responsiveness.
    *   **Multi-domestic Strategy**: Low Integration, High Responsiveness.
*   **Entry Modes** (Increasing Investment & Risk):
    *   Global Sourcing -> Exporting/Importing/Licensing/Franchising -> Strategic Alliance (JV) -> Foreign Subsidiary.
