Master Quiz 3: Environment & Strategy

Question 1 Lecture 2: Environment
Which of the following is an example of the General External Environment?
  • (a) A competitor's price cut
  • (b) A change in national tax laws
  • (c) A supplier delay
  • (d) A customer complaint
Correct Answer: (b)
Legal/Political factors like tax laws impact all industries and are part of the General Environment (PESTEL).
Question 2 Lecture 2: SWOT
In SWOT analysis, 'Opportunities' and 'Threats' refer to __________.
  • (a) internal factors
  • (b) financial ratios
  • (c) external factors
  • (d) past performance
Correct Answer: (c)
Strengths/Weaknesses are Internal; Opportunities/Threats are External.
Question 3 Lecture 2: VRIO
VRIO analysis is used to determine __________.
  • (a) macro-economic trends
  • (b) if a resource provides a sustainable competitive advantage
  • (c) employee satisfaction
  • (d) market share
Correct Answer: (b)
VRIO (Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, Organized) assesses internal resources for competitive advantage.
Question 4 Lecture 2: VRIO
If a resource is Valuable and Rare but NOT Inimitable (easy to copy), it provides __________.
  • (a) sustainable competitive advantage
  • (b) temporary competitive advantage
  • (c) disadvantage
  • (d) parity
Correct Answer: (b)
If competitors can copy it, the advantage is only temporary.
Question 5 Lecture 3: Planning
Which of these is NOT a characteristic of SMART goals?
  • (a) Specific
  • (b) Measurable
  • (c) Ambiguous
  • (d) Time-bound
Correct Answer: (c)
SMART stands for Specific, Measurable, Actionable/Attainable, Relevant, Time-bound. Ambiguity is bad.
Question 6 Lecture 3: Strategic Hierarchy
Corporate Strategy answers the question __________.
  • (a) How do we compete in this business?
  • (b) What businesses should we be in?
  • (c) How do we support the business?
  • (d) How to price the product?
Correct Answer: (b)
Corporate Strategy sets the overall direction (Growth, Stability, Retrenchment) for the portfolio.
Question 7 Lecture 3: Generic Strategies
According to Porter, a 'Cost Leadership' strategy seeks to __________.
  • (a) sell unique products at high prices
  • (b) operate with lower costs than competitors for a broad market
  • (c) focus on a tiny niche
  • (d) innovate technologically
Correct Answer: (b)
Cost Leadership = Low Cost + Broad Market.
Question 8 Lecture 3: Generic Strategies
A strategy that offers unique products that customers value and pay a premium for is called __________.
  • (a) Cost Leadership
  • (b) Differentiation
  • (c) Retrenchment
  • (d) Diversification
Correct Answer: (b)
Differentiation focuses on uniqueness (brand, quality, service).
Question 9 Lecture 4: PESTEL
The 'S' in PESTEL stands for __________.
  • (a) Strategic
  • (b) Socio-cultural
  • (c) Systems
  • (d) Sales
Correct Answer: (b)
Political, Economic, Socio-cultural, Technological, Environmental, Legal.
Question 10 Lecture 4: Porter's 5 Forces
Which of the following explains 'Threat of New Entrants'?
  • (a) Customers forcing prices down
  • (b) Suppliers raising costs
  • (c) Ease with which new competitors can enter the market
  • (d) Rivalry among existing firms
Correct Answer: (c)
High barriers to entry reduce the threat of new entrants.
Question 11 Lecture 4: Porter's 5 Forces
If 'Supplier Power' is high, it means __________.
  • (a) suppliers have few choices
  • (b) suppliers can demand higher prices or lower quality
  • (c) buyers can negotiate lower prices
  • (d) there are many substitute suppliers
Correct Answer: (b)
High supplier power squeezes industry profits.
Question 12 Lecture 2: Operational Plans
Operational plans typically have a __________ timeframe compared to strategic plans.
  • (a) longer
  • (b) shorter
  • (c) similar
  • (d) infinite
Correct Answer: (b)
Operational plans are short-term (daily/weekly/monthly) implementations of strategy.
Question 13 Lecture 3: Value Disciplines
Which Value Discipline focuses on total solution and strong customer relationship?
  • (a) Operational Excellence
  • (b) Product Leadership
  • (c) Customer Intimacy
  • (d) Cost Leadership
Correct Answer: (c)
Customer Intimacy tailors the product/service to the specific customer.
Question 14 Lecture 3: VUCA
In a VUCA environment, 'Volatility' refers to __________.
  • (a) lack of clarity
  • (b) rate of change and instability
  • (c) interconnected parts
  • (d) predictability
Correct Answer: (b)
VUCA: Volatile (Unstable), Uncertain, Complex, Ambiguous.
Question 15 Lecture 2: Internal Environment
The Internal Environment includes factors like __________.
  • (a) Interest rates
  • (b) Organization Culture and Employees
  • (c) Competitors
  • (d) Laws
Correct Answer: (b)
Culture, structure, and resources are internal.
Question 16 Lecture 3: BCG Matrix
In the BCG Matrix, a 'Star' has __________.
  • (a) High Share, High Growth
  • (b) Low Share, High Growth
  • (c) High Share, Low Growth
  • (d) Low Share, Low Growth
Correct Answer: (a)
Stars are market leaders in growing markets.
Question 17 Lecture 3: BCG Matrix
The recommended strategy for a 'Cash Cow' is to __________.
  • (a) invest heavily for growth
  • (b) milk it to fund other businesses (Stability)
  • (c) divest immediately
  • (d) close it down
Correct Answer: (b)
Cash Cows generate cash to support Stars or Question Marks.
Question 18 Lecture 3: Value Chain
In Porter's Value Chain, which is a Primary Activity?
  • (a) HR Management
  • (b) Technology Development
  • (c) Inbound Logistics
  • (d) Procurement
Correct Answer: (c)
Primary: Inbound, Operations, Outbound, Marketing/Sales, Service. Others are Support.
Question 19 Lecture 2: Strategic Management
Strategic Management involves Analysis, Formulation, Implementation, and __________.
  • (a) Hiring
  • (b) Evaluation/Control
  • (c) Marketing
  • (d) Production
Correct Answer: (b)
Evaluation/Control ensures the strategy is working.
Question 20 Lecture 4: Competitive Advantage
Sustainable Competitive Advantage is difficult for competitors to __________.
  • (a) understand
  • (b) imitate or copy
  • (c) see
  • (d) buy
Correct Answer: (b)
Inimitability is key to sustainability (VRIO).
