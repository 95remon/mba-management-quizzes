Master Quiz 2: Foundations & History

Question 1 Lecture 1: Intro
According to the lecture, management is primarily described as __________.
  • (a) a technical skill to master
  • (b) a mindset and decision-making process
  • (c) a strict set of rules
  • (d) controlling people
Correct Answer: (b)
The lecture emphasizes that management is about 'mindset' and 'decision making' to fit the situation.
Question 2 Lecture 1: Organization
Which of the following best describes the 'Process' component of an organization?
  • (a) The intangible elements like culture
  • (b) The technical part involving hierarchy and steps
  • (c) The psychological contract
  • (d) The external environment
Correct Answer: (b)
Processes refer to the 'technical part' such as hierarchy, steps, and lines of influence.
Question 3 Lecture 1: Open System
An organization is considered an 'Open System' because it __________.
  • (a) has open doors for all employees
  • (b) interacts with and is affected by the external environment
  • (c) shares all its secrets
  • (d) has no hierarchy
Correct Answer: (b)
Open systems interact with the external environment (Regulations, Economy) and affect the market.
Question 4 Lecture 1: Efficiency
Efficiency is best defined as __________.
  • (a) doing the right things
  • (b) doing things right (minimum waste)
  • (c) setting high goals
  • (d) achieving the objective
Correct Answer: (b)
Efficiency focuses on 'How' to achieve results with minimum waste (Resource usage).
Question 5 Lecture 1: Effectiveness
Effectiveness is concerned with __________.
  • (a) minimizing costs
  • (b) the methodological approach
  • (c) achieving the stated goals
  • (d) reducing time spent
Correct Answer: (c)
Effectiveness focuses on 'What' is achieved (Goal attainment).
Question 6 Lecture 1: PDCA
In the PDCA cycle, the 'Act' phase focuses on __________.
  • (a) implementing the plan
  • (b) checking results
  • (c) making adjustments or improvements
  • (d) setting the budget
Correct Answer: (c)
Act involved Change/Improvement based on the Check phase.
Question 7 Lecture 1: Levels
Middle managers primarily focus on __________.
  • (a) overall corporate strategy
  • (b) day-to-day operations
  • (c) divisional/sector objectives
  • (d) doing the actual work
Correct Answer: (c)
Middle managers (Sectors/Divisions) translate top goals for their units.
Question 8 Lecture 1: Reactive vs Proactive
A proactive management approach is characterized by __________.
  • (a) waiting for problems to arise
  • (b) agility and planning ahead
  • (c) ignoring external feedback
  • (d) rigidly sticking to old plans
Correct Answer: (b)
Proactive means planning ahead, adaptability, and high visibility to solve problems early.
Question 9 Lecture 1: Business Model Canvas
In the Business Model Canvas, 'Value Proposition' belongs to the __________.
  • (a) internal cost side
  • (b) external revenue side
  • (c) finance bottom line
  • (d) supplier network
Correct Answer: (b)
Value Proposition is the core offering to the Customer (External Side).
Question 10 Lecture 1: Uber Case
In the Uber case study, 'Drivers' are considered __________.
  • (a) only employees
  • (b) a key customer segment
  • (c) irrelevant to the model
  • (d) competitors
Correct Answer: (b)
Uber serves two segments: Passengers (who need rides) and Drivers (who need income).
Question 11 Lecture 12: Classical School
Scientific Management (Taylorism) focused primarily on __________.
  • (a) human relations
  • (b) process efficiency and productivity
  • (c) bureaucracy
  • (d) strategic vision
Correct Answer: (b)
Taylor focused on the 'One Best Way', time/motion studies, and efficiency.
Question 12 Lecture 12: Fayol
Henri Fayol is associated with __________.
  • (a) Time and Motion studies
  • (b) Administrative Principles (14 Principles)
  • (c) Bureaucracy
  • (d) Hawthorne Studies
Correct Answer: (b)
Fayol developed the 14 Principles of Management and defined the 5 functions.
Question 13 Lecture 12: Weber
Max Weber's concept of Bureaucracy emphasizes __________.
  • (a) personal friendships
  • (b) flexible roles
  • (c) impersonality and formal authority
  • (d) creativity
Correct Answer: (c)
Bureaucracy is based on rules, hierarchy, and impersonal relationships to ensure fairness.
Question 14 Lecture 12: Behavioral School
The Hawthorne Studies were significant because they revealed __________.
  • (a) lighting does not affect work
  • (b) money is the only motivator
  • (c) the influence of group behavior and social factors
  • (d) structures are more important than people
Correct Answer: (c)
Hawthorne Effect: Social/Group factors and attention from leaders influence productivity.
Question 15 Lecture 12: Theory X/Y
A Theory X manager believes employees __________.
  • (a) are creative and self-directed
  • (b) dislike work and need control
  • (c) seek responsibility
  • (d) are motivated by praise
Correct Answer: (b)
Theory X assumes employees are lazy and need coercion.
Question 16 Lecture 12: Theory Y
A Theory Y manager is likely to __________.
  • (a) micromanage every task
  • (b) empower employees and trust them
  • (c) punish mistakes severely
  • (d) ignore employee ideas
Correct Answer: (b)
Theory Y assumes employees are active, accountable, and can be empowered.
Question 17 Lecture 12: Quantitative Approach
The Quantitative Approach to management applies __________.
  • (a) psychology
  • (b) statistics and strict models
  • (c) history
  • (d) marketing
Correct Answer: (b)
It uses math, stats, and optimization models (e.g. for logistics).
Question 18 Lecture 12: Systems Theory
Systems Theory views the organization as __________.
  • (a) a machine
  • (b) a collection of interrelated parts
  • (c) a family
  • (d) a battlefield
Correct Answer: (b)
Organization is a system of interrelated parts working together.
Question 19 Lecture 12: Contingency Theory
Contingency Theory suggests that __________.
  • (a) there is one best way to manage
  • (b) management depends on the situation
  • (c) managers should ignore the environment
  • (d) planning is useless
Correct Answer: (b)
No single way is best; it depends on the situation (If X, then Y).
Question 20 Lecture 12: Contemporary
TQM (Total Quality Management) focuses on __________.
  • (a) rapid production only
  • (b) continuous improvement and customer needs
  • (c) cutting staff
  • (d) financial audits
Correct Answer: (b)
TQM is committed to continuous improvement and quality.
Question 21 Lecture 1: Perception
Perception is described as a process that __________.
  • (a) is always accurate
  • (b) is free from bias
  • (c) takes incomplete info and forms a judgment
  • (d) managers should ignore
Correct Answer: (c)
Perception processes incomplete data and is vulnerable to bias/stereotyping.
Question 22 Lecture 1: Management Skills
Katz identified three essential management skills: Technical, Human, and __________.
  • (a) Financial
  • (b) Conceptual
  • (c) Physical
  • (d) Digital
Correct Answer: (b)
The trio is Technical, Human (Interpersonal), and Conceptual.
Question 23 Lecture 1: Management Skills
Human (Interpersonal) skills are equally important for __________.
  • (a) only top managers
  • (b) only middle managers
  • (c) all levels of management
  • (d) only first-line managers
Correct Answer: (c)
While conceptual/technical needs vary, human skills are critical at all levels.
Question 24 Lecture 1: Efficiency vs Effectiveness
High Efficiency but Low Effectiveness means __________.
  • (a) Right goals, poor resource use
  • (b) Wrong goals, good resource use
  • (c) Right goals, good resource use
  • (d) Wrong goals, poor resource use
Correct Answer: (b)
You are doing the wrong thing (ineffective) but doing it very cheaply (efficient).
Question 25 Lecture 1: Governance
Governance is best described as part of the __________ system.
  • (a) Motivational
  • (b) Control
  • (c) Production
  • (d) Marketing
Correct Answer: (b)
Governance monitors performance and accountability (Control).
Question 26 Lecture 1: KPIs
KPIs (Key Performance Indicators) are used to __________.
  • (a) punish employees
  • (b) check and monitor progress
  • (c) guess the future
  • (d) create confusion
Correct Answer: (b)
KPIs reflect the 'What & How' and are used to monitor achievement.
Question 27 Lecture 12: Skills
Political skills in management involve __________.
  • (a) running for office
  • (b) building a power base and connections
  • (c) ignoring office politics
  • (d) technical coding
Correct Answer: (b)
Political skill is the ability to build connections and influence.
Question 28 Lecture 12: Roles
The 'Figurehead' role is part of Mintzberg's __________ roles.
  • (a) Informational
  • (b) Decisional
  • (c) Interpersonal
  • (d) Technical
Correct Answer: (c)
Figurehead, Leader, Liaison are Interpersonal roles.
Question 29 Lecture 12: Roles
The 'Resource Allocator' role is part of Mintzberg's __________ roles.
  • (a) Informational
  • (b) Decisional
  • (c) Interpersonal
  • (d) Technical
Correct Answer: (b)
Decisional roles: Entrepreneur, Disturbance Handler, Resource Allocator, Negotiator.
Question 30 Lecture 12: TQM
Which of these is NOT a principle of Total Quality Management?
  • (a) Continuous Improvement
  • (b) Customer Focus
  • (c) Employee Involvement
  • (d) Rigid Hierarchy
Correct Answer: (d)
TQM encourages empowerment and flexibility, not rigid hierarchy.
