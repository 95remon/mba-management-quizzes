## Pages 4-5: Types of Plans (Cont.)
3.  **Single Use Plans**: Project based. Start & End date. Specific deliverables.
4.  **Standing Plans**: Policies, Procedures (SOPs). Recurring activities.

- **5W-2H Framework (Core Pillars of any Plan)**:
    1.  **What** = Objectives / Results.
    2.  **Who** = Responsibility.
    3.  **When** = Time / Schedule.
    4.  **Where** = Scope / Location.
    5.  **Why** = Justification (Supported by SWOT/Data).
    6.  **How** = Actions / Strategies.
    7.  **How Much** = Cost (Resources).

## Pages 6-8: Control & SMART Goals
- **Control Measures**:
    1.  **Financial**: "How Much".
    2.  **Time**: "When".
    3.  **Clan Control**: "Who" (Behavior/Accountability).
    4.  **KPIs**: Quality, Capacity, etc.
- **SMART Objectives**:
    - **S**pecific (Scope).
    - **M**easurable (%).
    - **A**ctionable/Achievable.
    - **R**elevant/Realistic.
    - **T**imely (Deadline).
- **Plan for Control**: Determine KPIs during the *Planning* stage.
- **Frequency**: Pre-execution (Prevention), During (Correction), Post (Feedback).

## Pages 14-16: Porter's Generic Strategies
- **Two Dimensions**: Market Scope (Broad vs Narrow) and Competitive Advantage (Cost vs Differentiation).
1.  **Cost Leadership**:
    - Target: Broad Market.
    - Advantage: Low Cost.
    - Focus: Efficiency, Standardization, Cost Control.
    - Value Prop: Basics at best price.
    - Examples: Amazon, Zara, Dell, Walmart.
2.  **Differentiation**:
    - Target: Broad Market.
    - Advantage: Unique/Distinctive.
    - Focus: R&D, Innovation, Brand, Emotional connection.
    - Value Prop: Unique, Premium.
    - Example: Apple.
3.  **Focus Cost**: Niche + Low Cost (e.g., Ryanair).
4.  **Focus Differentiation**: Niche + Unique (e.g., Luxury brands).

## Page 21: Best-Cost & Blue Ocean
- **Best-Cost Provider Strategy**: Hybrid between Differentiation and Cost Leadership.
- **Blue Ocean Strategy**: Value Innovation.

## Pages 22-24: Red vs Blue Ocean
- **Red Ocean (Existing Market)**:
    - Fierce competition.
    - Fight for market share.
    - Trade-off between value and cost.
    - "Bloody" water.
- **Blue Ocean (New Market)**:
    - Create new demand.
    - Make competition irrelevant.
    - Create new market space.
    - Break the value-cost trade-off.
    - Example: Cirque du Soleil (implied/classic example), or shifting industry boundaries.
- **Shift**: Disruption in industry moves from Red to Blue.

## Page 25: Customer Journey & Value Prop
- **Customer Journey**: Purchase -> Delivery -> Use -> Supplement -> Disposal.
- **Value Proposition Attributes**: Productivity, Simplicity, Fun, Reduced Risk, Environmentally Friendly.
- **Mapping**: Identifying where to add value across the journey.

## Pages 26-28: Miles & Snow Typology (Strategic Moves)
- **4 Categories of Strategic Behavior**:
    1.  **Prospectors**: Innovators, First movers, Aggressive, Accept risks, Search for new markets (e.g., Tech startups).
    2.  **Analyzers**: Second movers, Mix of Prospector & Defender, Maintain current market while innovating moderately (e.g., Big tech exploring new tiers).
    3.  **Defenders**: Conservative, Protect current market, Focus on efficiency/stability, Avoid risk.
    4.  **Reactors**: No clear strategy, Reactive, Drifts with events (Fail state).

## Pages 29-30: Corporate Strategies (Growth) & Ansoff Matrix
- **Corporate Level Question**: "Direction" for "Growth".
- **Ansoff Matrix**:
    1.  **Market Penetration**: Existing Product + Existing Market. (Increase sales/usage, more marketing). Lowest Risk.
    2.  **Product Development**: New Product + Existing Market. (Innovation, modification).
    3.  **Market Development**: Existing Product + New Market. (New geography, new segments).
    4.  **Diversification**: New Product + New Market. Highest Risk. Related or Unrelated.

## Pages 31-32: Integration & Diversification
1.  **Integration Strategies** (Ownership of Value Chain):
    - **Vertical Integration**:
        - **Forward Integration**: Moving closer to the customer (e.g., Manufacturer opening retail stores).
        - **Backward Integration**: Moving closer to suppliers (e.g., Buying the raw material supplier to control quality/cost).
    - **Horizontal Integration**: Buying a competitor (e.g., Uber acquiring Careem).
2.  **Diversification Strategy** (Portfolio):
    - **Related (Concentric)**: Same industry/value chain synergy. Economy of Scale.
    - **Unrelated (Conglomerate)**: Different industry. Economy of Scope. (e.g., GE, Mitsubishi).

## Pages 33-34: Strategic Alliances & Portfolio Analysis (BCG)
- **Strategic Alliances**: Ecosystem of partners. Use when total control/ownership isn't critical.
    1.  **Contractual Agreement**: Non-equity. Weaker trust.
    2.  **Equity (Joint Venture)**: Shared ownership.
- **BCG Matrix (Boston Consulting Group)**: Managing Product Portfolio.
    - **Stars**: High Market Share / High Growth. (Build/Invest).
    - **Cash Cows**: High Market Share / Low Growth. (Hold/Harvest - generate cash).
    - **Question Marks**: Low Market Share / High Growth. (Decide - Invest to turn into Star or Divest).
    - **Dogs**: Low Market Share / Low Growth. (Divest/Liquidate).
