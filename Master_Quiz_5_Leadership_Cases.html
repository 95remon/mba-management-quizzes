Master Quiz 5: Leadership & Cases

Question 1 Lecture: Leadership
Leadership is defined as the process of __________.
  • (a) managing resources
  • (b) inspiring others to work hard to accomplish important tasks
  • (c) maintaining the status quo
  • (d) forcing compliance
Correct Answer: (b)
Leadership is about inspiration and influence, whereas Management is about planning/organizing.
Question 2 Lecture: Implied Pwr
Which type of power comes from a manager's official position in the hierarchy?
  • (a) Expert Power
  • (b) Referent Power
  • (c) Legitimate Power
  • (d) Coercive Power
Correct Answer: (c)
Legitimate power is based on formal authority.
Question 3 Lecture: Implied Pwr
Expert Power is based on __________.
  • (a) fear of punishment
  • (b) special knowledge or technical competence
  • (c) personal charisma
  • (d) rewards
Correct Answer: (b)
Expert power derives from what you know (e.g., a doctor or IT specialist).
Question 4 Lecture: Leadership Style
A Transformational Leader is characterized by __________.
  • (a) focusing only on rewards
  • (b) inspiring extraordinary effort and innovation through vision
  • (c) maintaining stability
  • (d) avoiding risks
Correct Answer: (b)
Transformational leaders (like Steve Jobs) inspire change and high performance.
Question 5 Lecture: Leadership Style
Transactional Leadership focuses on __________.
  • (a) vision and values
  • (b) directing tasks and rewarding performance (quid pro quo)
  • (c) emotional bond
  • (d) servant leadership
Correct Answer: (b)
Transactional is about the exchange: You do this work, I give you this pay.
Question 6 Lecture: Motivation
In Maslow's Hierarchy, which need must be satisfied before Social needs become dominant?
  • (a) Self-Actualization
  • (b) Safety Needs
  • (c) Esteem Needs
  • (d) Growth Needs
Correct Answer: (b)
The order is Physiological -> Safety -> Social -> Esteem -> Self-Actualization.
Question 7 Lecture: Motivation
According to Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory, 'Hygiene Factors' (like pay and working conditions) __________.
  • (a) motivate people to work harder
  • (b) prevent dissatisfaction but do not motivate
  • (c) cause burnout
  • (d) are irrelevant
Correct Answer: (b)
Hygiene factors prevent dissatisfaction. Only 'Satisfiers' (Achievement, Growth) truly motivate.
Question 8 Lecture: Motivation
Equity Theory suggests that motivation is affected by __________.
  • (a) absolute pay
  • (b) social comparison of rewards to inputs
  • (c) fear of punishment
  • (d) goal setting
Correct Answer: (b)
People compare their Input/Output ratio to others (Referents).
Question 9 Lecture: Control
The first step in the Control Process is __________.
  • (a) Measuring performance
  • (b) Establishing objectives and standards
  • (c) Taking corrective action
  • (d) Firing poor performers
Correct Answer: (b)
You cannot control without a standard to check against.
Question 10 Lecture: Control
Feedforward Control differs from Feedback Control because it __________.
  • (a) happens after the work is done
  • (b) happens before work begins (preventive)
  • (c) happens during the work
  • (d) relies on peers
Correct Answer: (b)
Feedforward = Preliminary (Inputs); Concurrent = Steering (Process); Feedback = Post-action (Outputs).
Question 11 Lecture: Control
Management by Exception focuses attention on __________.
  • (a) everything
  • (b) significant deviations from the standard
  • (c) only top performers
  • (d) routine tasks
Correct Answer: (b)
It saves time by focusing only on what is going wrong (or exceptionally right).
Question 12 Case: Ryanair
Ryanair's business model is a classic example of __________ strategy.
  • (a) Product Differentiation
  • (b) Cost Leadership
  • (c) Niche Focus
  • (d) Premium Service
Correct Answer: (b)
Ryanair strips all costs (no frills) to offer the lowest prices.
Question 13 Case: Ryanair
To achieve its strategy, Ryanair focuses heavily on __________.
  • (a) Customer Intimacy
  • (b) Operational Efficiency
  • (c) Luxury Brand Image
  • (d) Employee Perks
Correct Answer: (b)
Low turnaround times, secondary airports, and online booking maximize efficiency.
Question 14 Case: Amazon
Amazon's expansion from books to electronics to AWS Cloud Services is an example of __________.
  • (a) Concentration
  • (b) Diversification
  • (c) Retrenchment
  • (d) Liquidation
Correct Answer: (b)
Diversification involves entering new business areas.
Question 15 Case: Amazon
Amazon's focus on 'Customer Obsession' relates to which Value Discipline?
  • (a) Product Leadership
  • (b) Customer Intimacy (at scale)
  • (c) Operational Incompetence
  • (d) Bureaucracy
Correct Answer: (b)
Amazon aims to be 'Earth's most customer-centric company'.
Question 16 General
Which management function involves monitoring performance and correcting deviations?
  • (a) Planning
  • (b) Organizing
  • (c) Leading
  • (d) Controlling
Correct Answer: (d)
Controlling ensures plans are achieved.
Question 17 General
Effectiveness is to 'Doing the right things' as Efficiency is to __________.
  • (a) Doing things right
  • (b) Doing things fast
  • (c) Doing things later
  • (d) Doing things cheap
Correct Answer: (a)
Classic Drucker distinction: Effectiveness = Ends; Efficiency = Means.
Question 18 Leadership
Servant Leadership emphasizes __________.
  • (a) serving the leader's ego
  • (b) helping others use their talents to full potential
  • (c) autocratic control
  • (d) Strict hierarchy
Correct Answer: (b)
Servant leaders focus on the growth and well-being of their followers.
Question 19 Motivation
Goal Setting Theory implies that goals should be __________.
  • (a) easy to reach
  • (b) vague
  • (c) specific and challenging
  • (d) impossible
Correct Answer: (c)
Specific, Challenging (but attainable) goals motivate best.
Question 20 Structure
A 'Boundaryless Organization' seeks to __________.
  • (a) build higher walls
  • (b) eliminate internal and external barriers
  • (c) limit communication
  • (d) increase bureaucracy
Correct Answer: (b)
Jack Welch's concept: Removing barriers between levels, functions, and external partners.
