## Page 2: Planning and Strategy
- **Context**: Successful organizations understand and deal with the **Dynamic Environment** (External and Internal).
- **Key Challenge**: Failing to adapt to the changing world is a major mistake.

## Page 3: Decisions are Situational
- **Planning**: Long-term/Short-term objectives? Strategies? Difficulty of goals?
- **Organizing**: Number of direct reports? Centralization? Job design? Structure changes?
- **Leading**: Handling low motivation? Leadership style? Stimulating conflict?
- **Controlling**: Activities to control? How to control? Significant deviations? Info systems?

## Page 4: External Environment (VUCA)
- **VUCA Components**:
    - **V**olatility: Rate of change. (Speed, magnitude).
    - **U**ncertainty: Unclear about the present. (Unfamiliar territory).
    - **C**omplexity: Multiple key decision factors. (Interdependencies).
    - **A**mbiguity: Lack of clarity about meaning of an event. (Multiple perspectives).

## Page 5: VUCA Prime (Response)
- **Vision**: Vanquishes Volatility.
- **Understanding**: Undoes Uncertainty.
- **Clarity**: Cuts Complexity.
- **Agility**: Alleviates Ambiguity.
- **Goal**: Be "VUCA Proof".

## Page 6: Strategic Analysis (External - Step 1/2)
- **Macro-Environment**: Broad context. **PESTEL** (Political, Economic, Sociocultural, Technological, Environmental, Legal).
- **Micro-Environment**: Immediate environment. **Porter's 5 Forces** (Suppliers, Buyers, Rivals, Substitutes, New Entrants).
- **Enterprise**: Sits in the center, affected by all.

## Page 7: Strategic Analysis Process (Step 1)
- **Strategic Analysis**: Analyzing organization, environment, competitive position.
- **Flow**: Identify Mission/Objectives -> Analyze Org (Strengths/Weaknesses) & Environment (Opp/Threats) -> **SWOT Analysis** -> Formulate Strategies -> Implement -> Evaluate.

## Page 8: Types of Plans
- **Breadth**: Strategic (Broad) vs. Operational (Narrow).
- **Time Frame**: Long term (>3 years?) vs. Short term (<1 year).
- **Specificity**: Directional (Flexible) vs. Specific (Defined).
- **Frequency**: Single use (One time) vs. Standing (Ongoing policy).

## Page 9: Levels of Strategy & Problem Types
- **Problem Types**:
    - **Unstructured / Non-programmed decisions**: Top Managers.
    - **Structured / Programmed decisions**: Lower Managers.
- **Levels of Strategy**:
    - **Corporate Strategy**: What businesses are we in? (Corporation level).
    - **Business Strategy**: How do we compete? (SBU Level).
    - **Functional Strategy**: How do we support? (Finance, HR, Manufacturing, Marketing).

## Page 10: (Duplicate/Similar to Page 5?)
- **VUCA Proof**: Cycle of Vision, Understanding, Clarity, Agility.
- **Actions**:
    - Vision + Agility = Growth.
    - Understanding + Clarity = Insight.
    - Agility + Clarity = Innovation.

## Page 11: Competitive Strategies (Porter)
- **Two Dimensions**: Market Coverage (Broad vs Narrow) and Value Creation (Low Cost vs Differentiation).
- **Strategies**:
    1.  **Overall Low-Cost Provider**: Broad market, Lower cost. Curb non-essentials.
    2.  **Broad Differentiation**: Broad market, Unique attributes. Premium price.
    3.  **Focused Low-Cost**: Niche market, Lower cost.
    4.  **Focused Differentiation**: Niche market, Unique attributes.
    5.  **Best-Cost Provider**: Hybrid. Value for money.
