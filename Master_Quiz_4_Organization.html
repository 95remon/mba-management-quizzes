Master Quiz 4: Organization & Culture

Question 1 Lecture 5: Planning
Which plan covers the entire organization and long-term goals?
  • (a) Strategic Plan
  • (b) Tactical Plan
  • (c) Operational Plan
  • (d) Contingency Plan
Correct Answer: (a)
Strategic plans are broad, long-term, and cover the whole organization.
Question 2 Lecture 5: Planning
Tactical plans are primarily the responsibility of __________.
  • (a) Top Management
  • (b) Middle Management
  • (c) First-line Management
  • (d) Board of Directors
Correct Answer: (b)
Middle managers implement tactical plans to support the strategy.
Question 3 Lecture 7: Structure
Which organizational structure groups people based on similar skills or tasks?
  • (a) Divisional
  • (b) Matrix
  • (c) Functional
  • (d) Network
Correct Answer: (c)
Functional structure groups by function (Marketing, Finance, HR).
Question 4 Lecture 7: Structure
A disadvantage of the Functional Structure is __________.
  • (a) lack of specialization
  • (b) 'Chimneys' or silos where communication across functions is poor
  • (c) duplication of resources
  • (d) too much focus on products
Correct Answer: (b)
Functional silos (chimneys) hinder cross-functional communication.
Question 5 Lecture 7: Structure
Which structure combines functional and divisional approaches, creating a dual-reporting system?
  • (a) Team
  • (b) Matrix
  • (c) Network
  • (d) Virtual
Correct Answer: (b)
Matrix structure involves reporting to both a functional manager and a project/product manager.
Question 6 Lecture 7: Structure
A structure that uses a central core to coordinate a network of outside suppliers and contractors is called __________.
  • (a) Bureaucracy
  • (b) Network Structure
  • (c) Divisional Structure
  • (d) Mechanistic
Correct Answer: (b)
Network structures outsource many functions, keeping a small core.
Question 7 Lecture 7: Design
A Mechanistic design is characterized by __________.
  • (a) flexibility and decentralization
  • (b) rigid hierarchy, centralization, and many rules
  • (c) empowered teams
  • (d) wide spans of control
Correct Answer: (b)
Mechanistic = Bureaucratic (Rigid, Centralized, Formal).
Question 8 Lecture 7: Design
An Organic design is best suited for __________.
  • (a) stable environments
  • (b) uncertain and changing environments
  • (c) mass production
  • (d) government agencies
Correct Answer: (b)
Organic designs are adaptive and flexible, fitting uncertain environments.
Question 9 Lecture 8: Change
According to Lewin's model, the first stage of change is __________.
  • (a) Changing
  • (b) Refreezing
  • (c) Unfreezing
  • (d) Coercing
Correct Answer: (c)
Unfreezing prepares the organization for change (melting resistance).
Question 10 Lecture 8: Change
The 'Refreezing' stage involves __________.
  • (a) creating a crisis
  • (b) stabilizing the change to make it long-lasting
  • (c) implementing new process
  • (d) ignoring feedback
Correct Answer: (b)
Refreezing ensures the change becomes the new normal.
Question 11 Lecture 8: Change Strategy
Which change strategy relies on formal authority and punishment?
  • (a) Rational Persuasion
  • (b) Shared Power
  • (c) Force-Coercion
  • (d) Facilitation
Correct Answer: (c)
Force-Coercion uses power/threats (fast but low commitment).
Question 12 Lecture 8: Change Strategy
Which strategy builds the highest level of commitment (Internalization)?
  • (a) Force-Coercion
  • (b) Rational Persuasion
  • (c) Shared Power
  • (d) Top-down
Correct Answer: (c)
Shared Power (Participation) leads to true ownership of the change.
Question 13 Lecture 8: Culture
The 'Core Culture' consists of __________.
  • (a) office layout and dress code
  • (b) underlying values and beliefs
  • (c) stories and heroes
  • (d) symbols
Correct Answer: (b)
Core culture is the deeper level of values (Observable culture is symbols/stories).
Question 14 Lecture 8: Culture
What is the 'Glass Ceiling'?
  • (a) A transparent roof design
  • (b) An invisible barrier limiting advancement of women/minorities
  • (c) A type of open office
  • (d) A strategic vision
Correct Answer: (b)
It describes the difficulty for minorities to reach top levels despite qualifications.
Question 15 Lecture 7: Authority
Span of Control refers to __________.
  • (a) the number of managers in a company
  • (b) the number of subordinates reporting directly to a manager
  • (c) the size of the budget
  • (d) the length of the product line
Correct Answer: (b)
Wide span = many subordinates; Narrow span = few subordinates.
Question 16 Lecture 7: Authority
Empowerment involves __________.
  • (a) giving employees authority to make decisions
  • (b) taking power away from unions
  • (c) increasing executive pay
  • (d) centralizing decision making
Correct Answer: (a)
Empowerment delegates authority and responsibility to workers.
Question 17 Lecture 7: Job Design
Job Enrichment differs from Job Enlargement because it adds __________.
  • (a) more tasks of the same level
  • (b) planning and evaluating duties (depth)
  • (c) higher pay only
  • (d) shorter hours
Correct Answer: (b)
Enrichment adds vertical depth (responsibility); Enlargement adds horizontal breadth (variety).
Question 18 Lecture 6: Global Strategy
A Transnational Strategy seeks __________.
  • (a) global efficiency only
  • (b) local responsiveness only
  • (c) both global efficiency and local responsiveness
  • (d) neither
Correct Answer: (c)
Transnational tries to achieve the best of both worlds (Standardization + Adaptation).
Question 19 Lecture 6: Diversification
Growth by acquiring suppliers or distributors is called __________.
  • (a) Horizontal Integration
  • (b) Vertical Integration
  • (c) Unrelated Diversification
  • (d) Concentration
Correct Answer: (b)
Vertical integration moves up or down the supply chain (Suppliers = Backward; Distributors = Forward).
Question 20 Lecture 6: Coopetition
Coopetition is the strategy of __________.
  • (a) destroying competitors
  • (b) working with rivals on projects of mutual benefit
  • (c) ignoring rivals
  • (d) price fixing
Correct Answer: (b)
Coopetition = Cooperation + Competition.
Question 21 Lecture 7: Bureaucracy
Which of these is NOT a characteristic of Weber's Ideal Bureaucracy?
  • (a) Clear division of labor
  • (b) Personal relationships
  • (c) Formal hierarchy
  • (d) Rules and procedures
Correct Answer: (b)
Bureaucracy relies on Impersonality, not personal relationships.
Question 22 Lecture 8: Innovation
Product Innovation results in __________.
  • (a) new ways of doing things
  • (b) new or improved goods/services
  • (c) better management styles
  • (d) lower costs
Correct Answer: (b)
Product innovation (iPhone) vs Process innovation (Assembly line).
Question 23 Lecture 8: Resistance
Why do people resist change?
  • (a) Fear of the unknown
  • (b) Loss of control
  • (c) Lack of trust
  • (d) All of the above
Correct Answer: (d)
Resistance stems from uncertainty, fear, and loss of habit/security.
Question 24 Lecture 7: Delegation
Delegation involves assigning responsibility, granting authority, and creating __________.
  • (a) Confusion
  • (b) Accountability
  • (c) Distance
  • (d) Conflict
Correct Answer: (b)
The three elements are Responsibility, Authority, and Accountability.
Question 25 Lecture 7: Structure
A 'Tall' structure typically has __________.
  • (a) Wide span of control
  • (b) Narrow span of control and many levels
  • (c) No managers
  • (d) Few levels
Correct Answer: (b)
Tall structures have many hierarchical levels and narrow spans.
